Tips & Education

Learn smart money habits to grow your wealth

Master your finances

Expert tips and proven strategies to help you save more, spend wisely, and build wealth.

💡
Saving
🏦
Pay Yourself First
Before paying any bills or spending on anything, immediately transfer a fixed amount to your savings account. Treat saving like a non-negotiable expense.
💡 Rule: Save at least 20% of your income before spending
Budgeting
📊
The 50/30/20 Rule
Divide your after-tax income into three buckets: 50% for needs (rent, food, utilities), 30% for wants (entertainment, dining), and 20% for savings and debt repayment.
💡 Example: Rs 50,000 salary → Rs 25K needs, Rs 15K wants, Rs 10K savings
Investing
📈
Start Investing Early
The power of compound interest means that investing Rs 5,000/month starting at age 22 will give you significantly more than starting at 32. Time in the market beats timing the market.
💡 Start with SIP in index funds — even Rs 500/month makes a difference
Saving
🛍
The 24-Hour Rule
Before making any non-essential purchase over Rs 1,000, wait 24 hours. This simple rule eliminates impulse buying and helps you decide if you truly need something.
💡 Most impulse buys feel less urgent after sleeping on them
Debt
💳
Avalanche Method for Debt
List all your debts by interest rate. Pay minimum on all, but throw every extra rupee at the highest interest debt first. This saves the most money mathematically.
💡 Credit cards (36-40% APR) should always be paid off first
Budgeting
🎯
Zero-Based Budgeting
Assign every single rupee of your income a job. Income minus expenses should equal zero. This doesn't mean spend everything — savings and investments count as expenses too.
💡 Use FinTrack's budget feature to assign limits to each category
Investing
🔄
Automate Your Investments
Set up automatic transfers to your investment accounts on payday. When investing is automatic, you eliminate the temptation to spend that money and build wealth effortlessly.
💡 Set up SIP auto-debit on the 1st of every month
Saving
🚨
Build an Emergency Fund
Before investing, save 3-6 months of living expenses in a liquid account. This protects you from going into debt when unexpected expenses hit — medical bills, job loss, car repairs.
💡 Goal: Rs 1-2 Lakhs in an FD or liquid mutual fund
